BAPU
museum

The Bapu Museum is an archaeological museum located on the Bandar Road in Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh, India. It was established in 1887 as part of the celebrations of the Golden Jubilee of Queen Victoria and was initially known as The Victoria Jubilee Museum1. The museum has a unique collection of arts and artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and relics of Buddhist and Hindu relics, some of which date back to the 2nd and 3rd centuries12. The museum is maintained by the Archaeological department1. It was renamed in memory of the renowned film director, illustrator, cartoonist, and author Bapu2..
